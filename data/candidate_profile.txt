Candidate Profile — Yang Yang
===============================

## Who She Is

Yang Yang is a banking professional with over 8 years of experience at Bank of China Australia, across retail banking, personal lending, credit risk review, and risk governance. She is an Australian Permanent Resident with full working rights. She is currently returning to work after a career break and is ready for full-time employment.

She holds a Master of Finance (Western Sydney University) and a Bachelor of Accounting (Southern Cross University).

## Actual Work Experience

### Credit Risk Manager — Bank of China Australia, Sydney Head Office (2019–2023)

This is her most recent and most senior role. Key responsibilities:

- Conducted independent post-approval reviews of residential lending cases — assessed whether financial information, supporting documents, and approval rationale were complete, consistent, and aligned with internal lending standards
- Reviewed policy updates, process changes, and new business initiatives to identify risk impacts, control gaps, and areas requiring refinement before approval (risk in change)
- Acted as a key risk stakeholder for Personal Banking submissions — provided independent review, challenge, and recommendations
- Led end-to-end review activities including issue investigation, stakeholder discussion, and development of risk findings and recommendations
- Engaged and challenged stakeholders across Personal Banking, branches, and operational risk teams
- Participated in Credit Risk Management Committee — provided input on policy changes, portfolio risk matters, and key initiatives
- Monitored lending portfolio performance including arrears trends, loan composition, LVR distribution, and key risk indicators
- Conducted root cause analysis on emerging risk patterns and escalated key risks
- Contributed to quarterly APRA risk reporting
- Worked in a lean team with broad exposure across personal and business credit risk — strong ownership and independent execution

### Assistant Relationship Manager — Bank of China Australia, Hurstville Branch (2016–2019)

- Supported end-to-end lending processes: document collection, income and liability review, serviceability assessment, loan application preparation, and settlement coordination
- Collected and reviewed customer documents (payslips, bank statements, identification) for completeness and accuracy
- Worked closely with brokers, customers, and internal teams to progress applications
- Developed strong understanding of lending criteria, serviceability, and customer financial profiles

### Teller — Bank of China Australia, Hurstville Branch (2013–2015)

- Processed daily banking transactions with a focus on accuracy and compliance

## Her Key Strengths

The combination that makes Yang distinctive: she has both frontline lending experience (she knows how applications are built and submitted) AND independent risk review experience (she assessed whether those applications and decisions were sound). Most candidates have one or the other. This makes her well-suited to roles that sit at the intersection of lending and risk.

Specific strengths:
- Post-approval and hindsight review of lending cases
- Risk in change — reviewing policy and process changes before they go live
- Credit assurance and lending quality review
- Control gap identification and issue investigation
- Stakeholder engagement and challenge (she can push back on business teams)
- APRA regulatory awareness
- Portfolio monitoring, arrears analysis, risk reporting
- Practical understanding of residential lending, broker channels, and credit documentation
